What are the differences between relative, absolute, and fixed positioning?

relative

Like moving a sticker on a page. Imagine you have a sticker on a page. Normally, the sticker stays in its place. But if you use relative, you can move the sticker up, down, left, or right a little bit. The sticker still "belongs" to the page and keeps its spot.

absolute

Like floating a balloon in a box Now think of a balloon inside a box. If you say absolute, the balloon floats around inside the box wherever you tell it to go. But the balloon doesn’t take up space on the ground anymore—it just floats.

fixed

Like gluing a picture to the window Imagine you glue a picture to the window of your house. No matter how much you move or scroll, the picture stays stuck on the window. That’s what fixed does—it doesn’t move when you scroll the page.
